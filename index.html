Some Facts

While visiting in Vienna in 1781, he was dissmissed from his Salzburg position. He chose to stay in Vienna where he achieved fame but little financial security. During his final years in Vienna, he composed many of his best-known symphonies, concertos and operas and portions of the Requiem, which was largely unfinished at the time of his early death at the age of 35. The circumstances of his death have been much mytholized.

He composed more than 600 works, many of which are acknowleged as pinnacles of symphonic, concertrante, chamber, operatic and choral music. He is considered as one of the greatest clssical composers of all time acknowleged his influence on Western music is profound.
